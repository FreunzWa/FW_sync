# Common behavioural problems
- sleep issues
    + common, p/w trouble sleep onset, night wakings
    + ==parental sleep deprivation
    + rx
        * predictable routeines around bedtime
        * bedtime associations can be problematic (eg requires parent present) @nighttime waking == wakes up parents
        * manage waking @putting child back to bed and reassring them gently. must persist even @very difficult @crying. 
        * medications no persistent benefit. 
- food issues
    + v. unlikely child becomes ++malnourished @poor eating. 
    + rule out medical eg @oromotor, swallowing disorder or other developmental disorder
    + rx to reduce stress around food:
        * offer 3 meals and 3 snacks to minimise time bewteen eating opportunities
        * take the unfinished food away until next meal
        * allow sufficient but limited time for finishing the meal (eg 20 minutes)
        * give child 5 minute warning before food is taken away to give time to complete meal
        * DO NOT become upset
        * present food/drink @same environment eg high chair tray or low table.
- tantrum beahviours
    + managing troublesome tantrums
        * avoid triggers to tantrum
        * ignore tantrums
        * @tantrum == say nothing, avoid eye contact, dont convey to child that you are upset @will add fuel to fire
        * @disrupting/ antisocial behaviour == put them in isolated uninteresting place (Eg 1 min per year of age). tell to stay but only close door if they can get out. put them back in if the go out
        * positive reinforcement: praise when they are doing good things.

- Oppositional defiant disorder (6-10% prevalence)
    + persisent opposition, defiannt, disobedient and disruptive behaviour for at least 6 months. less violent than CD.
    + difficult to settle as infant, frequent tantrums as toddler, bullying and disruptive as a child. high risk of abuse from parents and dropping out of school (and developing conduct disorder)
    + development factors
        * child difficult temperament
        * difficult settle as baby
        * high motor activity
        * extreme emotional reactions
    + features
        * persistent testing of limits
        * deliberately annoying others
        * frequent tantrums/ losing temper
        * failing to accept responsibility
        * doesnt make frends easily
    + high assoc with ADHD (65% of ADHD have it)
    + rx
        * address any other cconditions (eg ADHD)/ leaerning difficulties
        * lessen stress in teh family
        * mental health plan and mental health assessment (allows family to access psychological services under the MBS) -- up to 12 sessions/ year'
        * medication: usu not used. @also ADHD then stimulants may help
- Conduct disorder
    + often bullies, initiates physical fights, physically cruel to people/ animals, has stolen, forced sexual activity, deliverate fire setting or destruction of property, is truant and runs away
- ADHD
    + most common neurodevelopmental disorder, 3-5%, more common @boy. some children have ADHD without the hyperactivity
    + Assessment
        * H
            - exacerbating factors - eg sleep deprivation/ nuitritional defecity
        * DSM (6+ months of Inattention / hyperactivity-impulsivity)
            - 6+ of Inattention (eg trouble with sustained concentration tasks, loses things necessary for tasks, easily distracted by extraneous stimuli, forgetful in daily activities, doesnt seem to be listening @spoken to, careless mistakes/ overlooks detrails)
            - 6+ of hyperactivity-impulsivity 
                + hyhperactivity (eg fidgets, leaves seat against command, climbs/ runs @inappropriate, difficult playing quietyl, uncomfortable @sit still for period, talks excessively)
                + impulsivity (blurts out answers to questions, cannot wiat for turn in conversation, take over what others are doing)
        * investigations
            - hearing assessment with formal audiology
            - edcuational psychologist @learning difficulties
        * treatment
            - see behaviour modficiatio below
            - medication: 
                + stimulant medication (1-2% children in australia are on it.). greatly improve concentration and reduce impulsivity an dhyperactivity.
                + work 80%
                + Methylphenidate (Ritalin) or dexamphetamine). either as short (few hours) or long acting (8-12 hours).
                + side effects: stomach aches, decreased appetite is most common, headaches, insomnia, increaesd irritability. %has effect on growth. may cause increased HR, so sometimes do tests @preivous cardiac condition/ family history.
                + monitor every 6 months, but more frequently at the start
                + fish oils may help a bit


# Behavioural management for ADHD/ ODD and similar disobeidnet
- school
    + instructions brief + clear, always tap on shoulder/ say name when giving important information. repeat instructions.
    + keep work area uncluttered, sit child at front next to good role model students
    + most important work done in teh rmoning @better concentration here
    + keep checklist for what need to do
    + reducing fatigue @having rest breasks (eg 5 mins per 30 mins study)
    + have a fixed routine
- home
    + postiive reinforcement for good behaviour (rewards chart/ token economy). change rewards frequently so does nt get bvorded with rewards
    + written down family rules
    + praise for when child is being good, and be specific about eh behaviours you want to encourage
    + ignore @minor attention seeking behaviours
    + consequences should be immediate, need to be relaistic. eg @young childrern should be a consequence that happens that day.
